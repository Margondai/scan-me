    <span class="section-tag">02 · Twice Exceptional</span>

    <h2 class="section-title">2E — Gifted & Different</h2>

    <p class="lead">Twice exceptional (2E) means being both intellectually gifted <em>and</em> having a learning difference or neurodevelopmental condition — at the same time.</p>

    <p>The "twice" refers to two exceptionalities existing together: extraordinary strengths in some areas, and significant challenges in others. 2E individuals often have <span class="highlight">asynchronous development</span> — excelling far beyond their peers intellectually while facing real struggles with focus, sensory processing, emotional regulation, or traditional learning environments.</p>

    <p>Because their gifts can mask their challenges (and vice versa), 2E people are frequently misunderstood, underserved, or simply exhausted from navigating a world not designed for them.</p>

    <blockquote>"Being twice exceptional means your brain runs powerful software on hardware that doesn't always cooperate."</blockquote>

  <section class="section">

    <span class="section-tag">03 · ADHD</span>

    <h2 class="section-title">ADHD — Not a Deficit of Attention</h2>

    <p class="lead">ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting how the brain regulates attention, impulse control, and executive function.</p>

    <p>The name is misleading. People with ADHD don't lack attention — they have <span class="highlight">variable, interest-driven attention</span>. Tasks that are novel, urgent, challenging, or personally meaningful can trigger deep, sustained hyperfocus. Routine or low-stimulation tasks are genuinely harder to engage with neurologically — not a matter of willpower.</p>

    <p>ADHD brains also tend to be highly creative, intuitive, and capable of making connections others miss — which makes the 2E combination particularly powerful (and particularly exhausting).</p>

    <blockquote>"ADHD is like having a race-car brain with bicycle brakes — the engine is extraordinary, the challenge is steering."</blockquote>
