01 · Extroversion

I Gain Energy From People

Extroversion isn't about being loud or the center of attention — it's about where your energy comes from.

Extroverts recharge by being around others. Conversation, collaboration, and social connection don't drain me — they fuel me. Solitude, while sometimes needed, slowly depletes my energy rather than restoring it.

An extrovert isn't someone who talks a lot — they're someone who thinks out loud, alongside others.

02 · Twice Exceptional

2E — Gifted & Different

Twice exceptional (2E) means being both intellectually gifted and having a learning difference or neurodevelopmental condition — at the same time.

The "twice" refers to two exceptionalities coexisting: extraordinary strengths in some areas, and significant challenges in others. 2E individuals often have asynchronous development — excelling far beyond peers intellectually while facing real struggles with focus, sensory processing, emotional regulation, or traditional learning environments.

Because their gifts can mask their challenges (and vice versa), 2E people are frequently misunderstood, underserved, or simply exhausted from navigating a world not designed for them.

Being twice exceptional means your brain runs powerful software on hardware that doesn't always cooperate.

🧠

Advanced Reasoning

Deep pattern recognition and cross-domain thinking.

🔍

Intense Curiosity

Hyperfocus on topics that light up the brain — real depth, not just breadth.

🎭

Invisible Struggles

High achievement can hide real daily challenges from the outside world.

03 · ADHD

ADHD — Not a Deficit of Attention

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental condition affecting how the brain regulates attention, impulse control, and executive function.

The name is misleading. People with ADHD don't lack attention — they have variable, interest-driven attention. Tasks that are novel, urgent, challenging, or personally meaningful can trigger deep, sustained hyperfocus. Routine or low-stimulation tasks are genuinely harder to engage with neurologically — not a matter of willpower.

ADHD brains also tend to be highly creative, intuitive, and capable of making connections others miss — which makes the 2E combination particularly powerful (and particularly exhausting).

ADHD is like having a race-car brain with bicycle brakes — the engine is extraordinary, the challenge is steering.
